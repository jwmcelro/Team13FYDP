Asynchronous Lectures

Ensure video lectures are uploaded in accessible formats that allow users to learn at their own pace.

Microsoft PowerPoint

This workflow includes the following task: sound effects, audio descriptions, closed captions.

1. Best practice: Avoid use of sound effects in lectures

Avoid using sound effects as it causes confusion for students with ADHD and hearing impairments.

Step 1: Select the Transition tab and check if underneath "Sound", it reads [No Sound].

Step 2: Repeat for each slide to ensure there are not any slides with sound effects.

2. Audio description for video embedded slides

Include a voiceover to describe the actions in a video to help students who are visually impaired.

Step 1: Select the "Slide Show" tab and click on "Record Slide Show".



Step 2: Verbally describe the actions taken in the video, click on the "Pause" button, and then "End Show" to stop recording.

3. Add closed captions to a video

Videos are commonly used to convey information during a lecture. Closed captions can be added to the video to ensure that students who are deaf or hard of hearing can learn the content in the video as well.

Step 1: Open the slide that has the video you want to add captions to.

Step 2: Select the video on the slide. A tab called "Playback" should appear on the top menu.

Step 3: On the "Playback" tab, click the "Insert Captions" button, and then select "Insert Captions".


Step 4: In the Insert Captions dialog, browse to your caption file. Select the file and then click "Insert".

Step 5: Play the video and check that the captions appear correctly.

YouTube

This workflow includes the following task: Youtube lecture transcription.

1. YouTube lecture transcription

YouTube automatically transcribes your videos and enables transcription exporting so you can upload the lecture transcription as supplementary material to aid students with ADHD who could be distracted by videos and students that are deaf or hard of hearing. In addition, YouTube videos have automatic closed captioning which benefits all students by providing additional context.

Step 1: Create and upload a lecture video to YouTube.

Step 2: While logged into your account, click "Edit Video."


Step 3: Click on the “Other features” link inside the edit dashboard that appeared.


Step 4: A new subtab will open up, select “Translation & transcription”.


Step 5: Click on the "English (Automatic)" link and then select "Edit".



Step 6: Modify the YouTube generated transcription to ensure it is accurate, in sync with the video, and includes closed captioning. After this, click on the "Actions" dropdown to export all the transcriptions to a file.


Step 7: Once all edits are made, click on the "Publish edits" button. Go to the YouTube video and click "Share" to get the Embed link to share with your students.


D2L Learn

This workflow includes the following task: Upload a lecture and/or video transcription file .

1. Upload a lecture and/or video transcription file

Hard of hearing or deaf students require closed captioning for lectures and videos. Further, often students prefer to read a transcript of a lecture over hearing the audio.

Step 1: When uploading a lecture, it is best practice to upload the transcript of the audio portions of the lecture. In addition, any videos that are posted should either have closed captioning or a transcription provided.
